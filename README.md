The rapid development of artificial intelligence, neurotechnologies, and cognitive augmentation systems is progressively transforming the relationship between human cognition and technological infrastructures. This transformation raises fundamental questions regarding the preservation of human autonomy, intellectual freedom, and decision-making sovereignty in environments increasingly influenced by algorithmic systems.
This article introduces the HUMROBOT analytical framework, a conceptual and systemic model designed to examine the evolution of the interaction between human cognition and intelligent technological systems. Building on previous work that introduced the concept of cognitive sovereignty, the present study proposes a structured model for analyzing the dynamics of hybridization between humans and technology as well as their implications for governance, ethics, and human autonomy.
The HUMROBOT framework conceptualizes the human–technology relationship as a dynamic system structured around three fundamental forces: technology, human cognition, and governance. The model also introduces a typology of hybrid cognitive conditions and proposes a governance structure aimed at preserving cognitive sovereignty while enabling responsible technological innovation.
By integrating contributions from the philosophy of technology, cognitive sciences, artificial intelligence ethics, and governance theory, this work aims to contribute to contemporary debates concerning the future of human autonomy in technologically augmented environments.
